I am a junior software/web developer with a background in computational physics.

I completed my master's degree in computational physics from San Diego State University in May 2015 where I was part of a collaboration working on a computational model of a neutron star. All of my coding was done in Fortran on a Linux machine.

In 2010, I was fortunate enough to be chosen for an internship at the European Organization of Nuclear Research (CERN) where I performed a study on the signal to noise ratio of the ATLAS experiment's pixel detector for the Large Hadron Collider.

Since graduating I have been teaching Mathematics, Physics and Computer Science at High Bluff Academy, a prestigious private high school in San Diego, California.

I am primarily self taught as a web developer and am currently working towards a Full Stack Development Certification through Free Code Camp, which has proven to be an excellent resource and aid to my learning. I am always eager to further my skillset and keep up with the latest technologies.
